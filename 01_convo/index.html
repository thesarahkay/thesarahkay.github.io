Dialogue in Literature

APR 2016

Designed and compiled by Sarah Kay Miller, with data cleaning by Derek Miller

How much of a book if conversation? Does it vary between nations? Does an author write the same proportion of dialogue throughout all of their novels? With a few lines of code, we were able to count how many words were in a novel, as well as how many words were in between quotation marks (this is what is considered "dialogue"). It turns out there is quite a spectrum of dialogue in classic novels, with the average proportion of conversation being 45%.

Texts were downloaded from gutenberg.org. Data cleaned using Python. Designed and compiled with Processing (Java) and InDesign. Read more about the process and methodology of this project at medium.com/@thesarahkay.
